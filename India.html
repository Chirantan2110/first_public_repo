About India

India, officially the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia. It's the world's most populous country and the seventh-largest by land area. India has a vast coastline of over 7,500 kilometers, bordering the Arabian Sea to the west, the Bay of Bengal to the east, and the Indian Ocean to the south.

importance

India is the birthplace of four major world religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism. It's also known for its rich and diverse cultural heritage, including its classical dances, music, and vibrant festivals like Diwali and Holi. India has made significant contributions to the world in mathematics, including the concept of zero and the decimal system, as well as in medicine, astronomy, and philosophy. It's home to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's oldest urban civilizations.

Places

Delhi

About Delhi Delhi, officially the National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT), is a city and a union territory of India. It is a metropolitan area that includes two distinct parts: Old Delhi, the historic walled city, and New Delhi, the modern capital of India, which serves as the seat of the Government of India. Delhi is situated on the banks of the Yamuna River and is one of the world's largest urban agglomerations.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum located on the south bank of the Yamuna River in the city of Agra, Uttar Pradesh. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The monument is the centerpiece of a vast complex that includes a mosque, a guesthouse, and a formal garden.

3.Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historical fort in Delhi, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The fort is known for its massive red sandstone walls and is a significant symbol of Mughal power and architectural brilliance.
